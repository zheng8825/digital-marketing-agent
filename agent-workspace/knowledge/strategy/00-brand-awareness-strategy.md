## 1. Objective & success definition
**Primary goal:** grow *brand awareness* and *qualified traffic* for ASUS notebooks in
Malaysia. Sales happen mostly through retail/e-tail partners (Shopee, Lazada, Senheng,
Harvey Norman, All IT, Thunder Match, etc.), so our job is **demand creation + handoff**,
measured by:

| Tier | KPI | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Awareness | Reach / unique reach, video views (≥3s & ≥15s), brand search volume (ASUS / Vivobook / Zenbook MY), social follower growth, share of voice | track monthly |
| Consideration | Sessions to asus.com/my notebook pages, time on page, scroll depth, "where to buy" clicks, product-page views, configurator/compare usage | GA4 |
| Handoff (proxy for sales) | Outbound clicks to partner store listings (Shopee/Lazada/retailer), promo-code impressions, retailer-tagged UTM traffic | the bridge to sell-through |

Pick **one north-star per quarter** (suggest: *qualified notebook sessions on asus.com/my*) plus 2–3 supporting KPIs.

## 2. Audience (Malaysia) — starting segmentation
1. **Students / fresh grads (18–24)** — affordability, portability, "good enough" performance, campus credibility. Hero: Vivobook, Vivobook Go, Chromebook. Channels: TikTok, IG Reels, campus KOLs, YouTube shorts.
2. **Young professionals / creators (25–34)** — design, battery, OLED screen, light + powerful, work-from-anywhere. Hero: Zenbook, Zenbook OLED, ProArt (for creators). Channels: IG, YouTube, tech reviewers, LinkedIn (lite).
3. **Gamers / enthusiasts (16–28)** — performance, GPU, refresh rate, RGB, esports culture. Hero: ROG, TUF Gaming. Channels: YouTube, TikTok, FB gaming groups, Twitch/streamers, esports tie-ins. *(Likely already well served — coordinate, don't duplicate.)*
4. **SOHO / SME owners (30–45)** — reliability, warranty, value, "business" trim. Hero: ExpertBook, Vivobook. Channels: FB, Google Search, LinkedIn.
5. **Family / gift buyers (parents 35–50)** — buying for kids/students, trust + service network + price. Channels: FB, Google Search, retailer co-marketing.

Languages: campaign-level mix of **English + Bahasa Melayu + Chinese**; creative localised, not just translated. Festive moments matter (Raya, CNY, Deepavali, Merdeka, Christmas) plus **back-to-school (Jan & ~June/July intake)** and **mega-sales (3.3, 6.6, 9.9, 11.11, 12.12)**.

## 3. Positioning / message house (draft)
- **Master brand line:** "ASUS — In Search of Incredible" (global) → MY expression: incredible *for your life here* — study, hustle, create, game.
- **Sub-brand wedges:**
  - Vivobook → *"Everyday brilliance, everyday price."* (value + colour + OLED option)
  - Zenbook → *"Powerful. Beautiful. Weightless."* (premium thin-and-light, OLED, AI PC)
  - ProArt → *"Built for creators."* (colour-accurate, certified)
  - ROG / TUF → *"Game like it's your job."* / *"Tough enough."*
  - ExpertBook → *"Business, handled."* (reliability + warranty + support)
- **Always-on proof points:** ASUS AI PC / Copilot+ where applicable, OLED screens, military-grade durability (TUF/ExpertBook), local warranty & service centres, awards/reviews.

## 4. Channel plan (always-on + bursts)
**Always-on (awareness + traffic engine):**
- **YouTube** — hero product films + reviewer seeding + shorts; primary for consideration.
- **Meta (FB + IG)** — Reels/Stories for reach & video views; carousel/collection driving to product pages; retargeting site visitors → "where to buy".
- **TikTok** — short-form, creator-led, trend-jacking; strongest for student/gamer reach in MY.
- **Google** — Search (brand defense + "asus laptop", "best laptop under RM3000", "laptop for students" etc.) + Performance Max / Demand Gen for reach; YouTube as inventory.
- **SEO / owned** — keep asus.com/my notebook pages crawlable, fast, and useful (compare tool, "which laptop for me" quiz, buying guides, blog: "best ASUS laptop for students 2026", "OLED vs IPS"). This compounds traffic for free.
- **Organic social** — consistent calendar, community management, UGC reposts.
- **CRM/email** — even if list is small, start a newsletter (new launches, deals, tips) — owned reach for future.

**Bursts (campaign moments):**
- New product launches (NUC of attention) — teaser → reveal → review wave → sustain.
- Mega-sales & festive — co-marketing with retailers (shared UTMs, shared creative).
- Back-to-school — student bundles, campus activations, KOL "what's in my bag".
- Esports / gaming events — ROG/TUF tie-ins.

**KOL / creator tiers:** 1 macro/tech-authority (credibility) + 5–10 mid-tier (category fit: study, creator, gaming, lifestyle) + 15–30 micro/nano (authenticity, BM/Chinese/regional). Brief them, give UTMs + promo codes, measure.

## 5. Content pillars (for the calendar)
1. **Product hero** — beauty shots, key features, "why this one".
2. **Use-case stories** — "a day with Zenbook for a freelance designer", "Vivobook through finals week", "ROG on a budget gaming setup".
3. **Comparison / buying help** — model vs model, ASUS vs generic "other brands", spec explainers (OLED, AI PC, RAM/SSD), price-tier guides.
4. **Social proof** — reviews, awards, UGC, testimonials, "Malaysians love…".
5. **Deals & where-to-buy** — promos, bundles, retailer links, festive offers.
6. **Brand / culture** — sustainability, innovation, local community, esports, campus.

Cadence (suggested): 4–6 organic posts/week/platform, 1 hero content piece/month, weekly reviewer/UGC amplification, paid always-on behind the best 20%.

## 7. 90-day rollout (assuming approval & budget land)
- **Weeks 1–2 — Foundation:** finalize SKUs & budget; lock UTM + GA4 events; audit asus.com/my notebook pages & social; competitor & SoV baseline; brief creative; shortlist KOLs.
- **Weeks 3–6 — Launch always-on:** publish buying guides + compare/quiz on site; switch on YouTube + Meta + TikTok + Google always-on (reach + traffic objectives); start organic calendar; first KOL wave; set up dashboards.
- **Weeks 7–10 — First burst:** align to nearest moment (e.g. mid-year back-to-school / 6.6) — student/creator push, retailer co-marketing, heavier KOL, retargeting.
- **Weeks 11–13 — Read & optimise:** monthly review; double down on top channels/creatives/content; brand-lift read; plan next quarter; document learnings in `campaigns/`.
